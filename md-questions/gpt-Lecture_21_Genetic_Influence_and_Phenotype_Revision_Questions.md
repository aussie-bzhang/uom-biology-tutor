# **Reference Answers**

*Answers are concise model answers. For short-answer and application questions, equivalent explanations should receive credit if the genetic reasoning is correct.*

## **Practice Set 1**

A. Short-answer questions

**1.** Ploidy is the number of sets of chromosomes. Haploid cells have one set (n); diploid cells have two sets (2n), usually as homologous pairs.

**2.** DNA replication produces two sister chromatids for each chromosome, but sister chromatids remain joined as one chromosome until they separate during cell division.

**3.** A locus is a position of a gene or DNA sequence on a chromosome. An allele is a variant form of that sequence. Diploids usually have two homologous chromosomes, so they usually carry two alleles at each autosomal locus.

**4.** Genotype is genetic composition, such as AA, Aa or aa. Phenotype is the observed or measured biological property, such as enzyme activity, pigment level or coat colour.

**5.** In incomplete dominance, one functional allele produces an intermediate amount of product. The heterozygote has an intermediate phenotype because one allele dose is not enough to produce the full homozygous phenotype.

B. Yes/No questions

**1.** No. It is one replicated chromosome with two sister chromatids.

**2.** Yes.

**3.** No. Some alleles are silent, intergenic or otherwise do not alter protein sequence.

**4.** Yes.

**5.** Yes.

C. Multiple-choice questions

1. B, 2. B, 3. C, 4. C, 5. B, 6. C, 7. C, 8. D, 9. B, 10. A

D. Biochemical and application questions

**1.** Incomplete dominance. RR has two functional allele doses and high pigment; rr has no functional dose and no pigment; Rr has one dose and intermediate pigment, producing pink.

**2.** Four chromosomes and eight chromatids. Chromosome number remains four; each chromosome consists of two sister chromatids after replication.

**3.** Phenotype AB; genotype IAIB. It is codominance because both A and B allele products are expressed as detectable antigens.

A. Short-answer questions

**1.** Variation may be intergenic, silent, conservative, or below a phenotypic threshold; environmental and regulatory context can also influence whether a change is visible.

**2.** Homozygous means two identical alleles; heterozygous means two different alleles; hemizygous means only one allele copy at a locus, commonly for some sex-linked loci in XY individuals.

**3.** It is still a DNA sequence variant at the locus, even if codon degeneracy means the amino acid sequence is unchanged.

**4.** In complete dominance, the heterozygote resembles one homozygote. In incomplete dominance, the heterozygote is intermediate, often because product amount is intermediate.
