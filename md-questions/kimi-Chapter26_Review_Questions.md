# Question 1 (10 marks)

***Chicken Comb Genetics - Additive Traits***

In chickens, comb shape is determined by two independently assorting genes: the Rose gene (R/r) and the Pea gene (P/p). The dominant R allele alone produces a Rose comb; the dominant P allele alone produces a Pea comb. When both dominant alleles are present (R\_ P\_), the result is a Walnut comb. When both are recessive (rr pp), a Single comb results.

**(a)** A breeder crosses a true-breeding Rose-combed chicken with a true-breeding Pea-combed chicken. What are the genotypes and phenotypes of the F1 generation? (2 marks)

**(b)** If two F1 individuals are crossed, what phenotypic ratio is expected in the F2 generation? Show your working using a Punnett square. (4 marks)

**(c)** In an actual experiment, a breeder obtains 160 progeny from an F1 x F1 cross. Calculate the expected number of chickens in each phenotypic class. (2 marks)

**(d)** Explain why this is classified as an additive trait rather than a case of dominant/recessive epistasis. (2 marks)

# Question 2 (10 marks)

***Drosophila Wing Development - Gene Interaction Pathways***

In Drosophila, wing development requires two genes working in a pathway: the wingless gene (wa) encodes a signalling protein (Wg) that establishes wing tissue identity, and the vestigial gene (vg) encodes a transcription co-activator (Vg) required for wing development. The wild-type alleles are wa+ and vg+. A cross between true-breeding wingless (wa/wa ; vg+/vg+) and true-breeding vestigial (wa+/wa+ ; vg/vg) flies produces all wild-type F1 progeny. The F2 from an F1 intercross shows a 9:3:4 phenotypic ratio (wild-type : vestigial : wingless).

**(a)** Write the genotypes of the P generation parents. Explain why the F2 ratio is modified from the standard 9:3:3:1 dihybrid ratio. (3 marks)

**(b)** Draw a biochemical pathway diagram showing how the products of these two genes interact during wing development. Label which step is affected in each mutant. (3 marks)

**(c)** What proportion of the F2 progeny would be homozygous recessive at both loci? What is their phenotype? Explain your answer. (2 marks)

**(d)** If a wingless fly from the F2 is test-crossed, what phenotypic ratio would you expect among the offspring? (2 marks)

# Question 3 (10 marks)

***Flower Development - Epistasis in Arabidopsis***

In Arabidopsis thaliana, flower development is controlled by multiple genes including LEAFY (LFY) and AGAMOUS (AG). LFY is required for establishing floral meristem identity (converting vegetative shoot to flower). AG is required for differentiation of reproductive floral organs (stamens and carpels). The lfy mutant lacks flowers entirely; the ag mutant has indeterminate flowers with petals replacing reproductive organs. Both single mutants are sterile.

**(a)** Two heterozygous plants (AG/ag ; LFY/lfy) are crossed. Using a branch diagram or Punnett square, determine the expected phenotypic ratio in the progeny. Describe the phenotype of each class. (4 marks)

**(b)** Identify which gene is epistatic in this pathway and explain why. In your answer, describe the molecular basis of the epistatic relationship between LFY and AG. (3 marks)

**(c)** A researcher discovers a double mutant (ag/ag ; lfy/lfy). Predict its phenotype and justify your prediction based on the epistatic relationship. (3 marks)

# Question 4 (10 marks)

***Coat Colour Inheritance - Modifier Genes and Epistasis***

Coat colour in mammals involves multiple interacting genes. In cats, the Black/Brown locus (B/b) controls pigment type: B\_ produces black pigment, while bb produces brown pigment. A second locus, the Dense/Dilute locus (D/d), acts as a modifier: D\_ allows full pigment deposition, while dd dilutes the pigment colour. Additionally, a third locus, the Extension locus (E/e), controls whether pigment is deposited in the coat at all: E\_ allows pigment expression, while ee results in a cream/yellow coat regardless of the B or D genotypes.

Genotype-to-phenotype summary:

| **E locus** | **B locus** | **D locus** | **Phenotype** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| E\_ | B\_ | D\_ | Black |
| E\_ | B\_ | dd | Blue (diluted black) |
| E\_ | bb | D\_ | Brown |
| E\_ | bb | dd | Lilac (diluted brown) |
| ee | \_\_ | \_\_ | Cream / Yellow |

**(a)** A pure-breeding black cat (EE BB DD) is crossed with a pure-breeding cream cat (ee bb dd). What is the phenotype and genotype of the F1 generation? (2 marks)

**(b)** Two F1 cats are intercrossed (F1 x F1). What is the expected phenotypic ratio in the F2 generation? Show all working. (5 marks)

**(c)** What proportion of the black-coated F2 cats would be expected to be true-breeding (homozygous at all three loci)? Explain your calculation. (3 marks)

# Question 5 (10 marks)

***Experimental Design - Genetic Analysis of a Novel Trait***

A plant geneticist discovers a new species of flowering plant with three distinct petal colour phenotypes: purple, red, and white. Initial crosses yield the following results:

Cross 1: Purple x White -> All purple F1

Cross 2: F1 x F1 -> 9 Purple : 3 Red : 4 White

Cross 3: Red x White -> 1 Red : 1 White

**(a)** Based on the F2 ratio from Cross 2, how many genes are involved in controlling petal colour? Explain your reasoning. (2 marks)

**(b)** Propose a biochemical pathway and gene interaction model that explains the 9:3:4 ratio. Assign gene symbols and identify which gene is epistatic. (3 marks)

**(c)** Write the genotypes of the parental plants in Cross 1 and verify your model by showing the expected F2 genotypes. (3 marks)

**(d)** Design a test cross that would allow you to distinguish between a purple-flowered plant that is homozygous dominant at both loci and one that is heterozygous at one or both loci. Predict the outcomes for each case. (2 marks)

## Question 1 - Chicken Comb Genetics

**(a)** P: RR pp (Rose) x rr PP (Pea). F1 genotype: All Rr Pp. F1 phenotype: All Walnut comb (2 marks)

**(b)** F1 x F1 cross: Rr Pp x Rr Pp. Using a 4x4 Punnett square with gametes RP, Rp, rP, rp from each parent. F2 phenotypic ratio: 9 Walnut (R\_ P\_) : 3 Rose (R\_ pp) : 3 Pea (rr P\_) : 1 Single (rr pp). (4 marks)

**(c)** Expected numbers from 160 progeny: Walnut = 9/16 x 160 = 90; Rose = 3/16 x 160 = 30; Pea = 3/16 x 160 = 30; Single = 1/16 x 160 = 10. (2 marks)

**(d)** This is an additive trait because each dominant allele contributes independently to the phenotype. The R and P alleles each produce distinct, non-overlapping effects that combine when both are present. Neither allele masks the other -- their effects sum to produce a novel phenotype (Walnut). This differs from dominant epistasis where one dominant allele masks the effect of another gene. (2 marks)

## Question 2 - Drosophila Wing Development

**(a)** P genotypes: wa+/wa+ ; vg/vg (vestigial, no functional Vg) x wa/wa ; vg+/vg+ (wingless, no Wg signal). The F2 ratio is modified because the wa/wa genotype (wingless) is epistatic to the vg/vg genotype. When no wing tissue is established (no Wg signal), the Vg protein cannot function regardless of its genotype. Therefore, both wa/wa vg+/vg+ and wa/wa vg/vg individuals are wingless, collapsing the expected 3:1 ratio for the bottom row into a single class. (3 marks)

**(b)** Pathway: Wg protein -> establishes Wing tissue identity -> Vg protein -> promotes Wing development. In wa mutant: no Wg protein, so wing tissue identity is not established (blocked at step 1). In vg mutant: Wg is present but no Vg protein, so wing tissue identity is established but development cannot proceed (blocked at step 2). Epistasis occurs because Wg acts upstream of Vg in the pathway. (3 marks)

**(c)** Homozygous recessive at both loci (wa/wa ; vg/vg) = 1/16 of F2. Phenotype: Wingless. This is because the wa mutation (no Wg signalling) prevents wing tissue identity from being established, making the vg genotype irrelevant -- the upstream block determines the phenotype. (2 marks)

**(d)** A wingless F2 fly could be: wa/wa ; vg+/vg+ or wa/wa ; vg+/vg. Test cross with wa+/wa+ ; vg/vg (tester). If wingless F2 is wa/wa ; vg+/vg+: offspring = 1/2 wild-type : 1/2 wingless. If wingless F2 is wa/wa ; vg+/vg: offspring = 1/4 wild-type : 1/4 vestigial : 1/2 wingless. (2 marks)

## Question 3 - Flower Development

**(a)** AG/ag ; LFY/lfy x AG/ag ; LFY/lfy cross. Expected F2 phenotypic ratio: 9 Normal flowers (AG/\_ ; LFY/\_) : 3 Indeterminate flowers (AG/\_ ; lfy/lfy) : 4 No flowers (ag/ag ; LFY/\_ or ag/ag ; lfy/lfy). The phenotypes: Normal = normal floral organs with proper stamens and carpels; Indeterminate = petals replacing stamens and carpels with repeated whorls; No flowers = vegetative shoots only (lfy mutants completely lack floral meristems). (4 marks)

**(b)** LFY is epistatic to AG. LFY acts upstream in the pathway by establishing floral meristem identity -- converting vegetative meristem to floral meristem. Only after a floral meristem is established can AG function to specify reproductive organs. If LFY is mutant (lfy/lfy), no floral meristem forms, so AG can never be expressed regardless of its genotype. This is recessive epistasis at the pathway level: the lfy mutation masks any effect of the ag mutation. (3 marks)

**(c)** The ag/ag ; lfy/lfy double mutant would show the lfy phenotype: completely lacking flowers (vegetative shoots only). This is because LFY acts upstream of AG in the pathway. The lfy mutation masks any effect of the ag mutation. Even though AG is also mutant, the more severe upstream block (no floral meristem) determines the final phenotype. This confirms LFY is epistatic to AG. (3 marks)

## Question 4 - Coat Colour Inheritance

**(a)** F1 genotype: All Ee Bb Dd. F1 phenotype: All Black (E allows pigment expression, B produces black pigment, D allows full deposition). (2 marks)

**(b)** F1 x F1: Ee Bb Dd x Ee Bb Dd. Since E is epistatic to B and D, first consider E locus: 3/4 E\_ (pigmented) : 1/4 ee (cream). Among the 3/4 pigmented: B locus gives 3/4 B\_ (black pigment) : 1/4 bb (brown pigment); D locus gives 3/4 D\_ (dense/full) : 1/4 dd (dilute). Combining B and D among pigmented cats: Black (B\_ D\_) = 3/4 x 3/4 = 9/16 of pigmented; Blue/Black-dilute (B\_ dd) = 3/4 x 1/4 = 3/16; Brown (bb D\_) = 1/4 x 3/4 = 3/16; Lilac/Brown-dilute (bb dd) = 1/4 x 1/4 = 1/16. Overall F2 ratio: 27/64 Black : 9/64 Blue : 9/64 Brown : 3/64 Lilac : 16/64 Cream, which simplifies to 27:9:9:3:16. (5 marks)

**(c)** True-breeding black cats must be EE BB DD. From the F2, probability = P(EE) x P(BB) x P(DD) = 1/4 x 1/4 x 1/4 = 1/64. Proportion of black cats that are true-breeding = (1/64) / (27/64) = 1/27. (3 marks)

## Question 5 - Experimental Design

**(a)** Two genes are involved. The modified dihybrid ratio 9:3:4 is characteristic of recessive epistasis, where the homozygous recessive genotype at one locus masks the expression of the other locus. This is a variation of the standard 9:3:3:1 ratio expected for two independently assorting genes. (2 marks)

**(b)** Proposed pathway: Gene P (dominant P\_ required): Colourless precursor -> Red intermediate. Gene E (dominant E\_ required): Red intermediate -> Purple pigment. When ee (homozygous recessive at E), the red intermediate accumulates and cannot be converted to purple, giving red phenotype. When pp (homozygous recessive at P), no red pigment is produced, giving white regardless of E genotype. The p allele shows recessive epistasis -- pp masks the effect of E. Model: P\_ E\_ = Purple; P\_ ee = Red; pp E\_ = White; pp ee = White. (3 marks)

**(c)** Cross 1 parents: PP EE (Purple) x pp ee (White). F1: All Pp Ee (Purple). F2 from Pp Ee x Pp Ee: 9 P\_ E\_ (Purple) : 3 P\_ ee (Red) : 3 pp E\_ (White) + 1 pp ee (White) = 9:3:4. This matches the observed ratio, confirming the model. (3 marks)

**(d)** Test cross: Cross the unknown purple plant with a double homozygous recessive tester (pp ee, white). If purple plant is PP EE: all offspring will be Pp Ee (all purple). If purple plant is Pp EE: offspring = 1/2 Pp Ee (purple) : 1/2 pp Ee (white). If purple plant is PP Ee: offspring = 1/2 Pp Ee (purple) : 1/2 Pp ee (red). If purple plant is Pp Ee: offspring = 1/4 Pp Ee (purple) : 1/4 Pp ee (red) : 1/4 pp Ee (white) : 1/4 pp ee (white) = 1 purple : 1 red : 2 white. (2 marks)
