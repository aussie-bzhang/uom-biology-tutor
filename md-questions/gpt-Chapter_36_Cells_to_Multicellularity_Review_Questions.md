# Reference Answers

## Set 1

### Short-answer

1. A tissue is a group of structurally related cells, usually with common developmental origin, acting as a functional unit. Tissues allow specialised cells to cooperate efficiently.
2. Epithelial: covering/lining/secretion/absorption; connective: support, transport, storage, protection; muscle: contraction/movement; nervous: information processing and signalling.
3. Squamous cells are thin and suited to diffusion/lining; cuboidal cells are cube-like and often secretory/absorptive; columnar cells are tall and suited to absorption or secretion. Layer number also affects protection versus exchange.
4. Whole muscle contains bundles of muscle fibres; each fibre is a multinucleate cell with many myofibrils; myofibrils contain repeating ordered actin and myosin filaments that form sarcomeres and create striations.
5. The intestine includes epithelial tissue for absorption/secretion, smooth muscle for movement, connective tissue for support, and nervous tissue for coordination and sensory control.

## Set 2

### Short-answer

1. Animal tissues derive from germ layers: ectoderm, mesoderm, endoderm. Plant tissue systems arise from primary meristems: protoderm, ground meristem, and procambium.
2. Alveoli are epithelial exchange surfaces associated with lung lining, while capillaries are vascular structures. Their close contact allows rapid gas exchange and shows integration of tissues from different developmental origins.
3. Respiratory system loads O2 and removes CO2; digestive system supplies nutrients and water; endocrine glands release hormones into blood; excretory system removes nitrogenous waste. The circulatory system transports these materials/signals.
4. Dermal tissue covers/protects and helps gas exchange; ground tissue performs metabolism such as photosynthesis and support; vascular tissue transports water, minerals, and sugars.
5. A leaf has dermal tissue including epidermis/cuticle/stomata, ground tissue including mesophyll for photosynthesis, and vascular tissue in veins for xylem and phloem transport.

### Applications

1. Alveolar lining is associated with epithelial tissue derived from endodermal lung lining, while capillaries/blood vessels are mesoderm-derived. Efficient gas exchange requires both exchange epithelium and vascular transport.
2. Removing the cuticle should increase water loss because the protective dermal barrier is reduced. The dermal tissue system is primarily affected.

## Set 3

### Short-answer

1. Example: enterocytes are cells; epithelial tissue forms absorptive lining; the small intestine is an organ; the gastrointestinal system is an organ system; all coordinate within the organism.
2. Meristematic cells form primary meristems, which form dermal, ground, and vascular tissue systems; these build organs such as leaves, stems, and roots; root and shoot systems form the whole plant.
3. Xylem transports water and minerals mainly upward; phloem transports sugars and other solutes between sources and sinks. Both are needed to connect photosynthesis, growth, and resource acquisition.
4. Flowers, fruits, and cones are organised structures made from specialised tissues and organs for reproduction and dispersal, so they are part of the plant body hierarchy.
5. Animals coordinate via circulatory, respiratory, digestive, endocrine, excretory, nervous, and muscular systems. Plants coordinate root and shoot systems via vascular tissue, hormones, and tissue integration to meet metabolic needs.
