# Answer Key

## Practice Set 1 - A. Short-answer answers

**1.** A macromolecule is a large biological molecule, often a polymer made from many repeating subunits called monomers. Monomers are linked together to form polymers.

**2.** Condensation joins monomers by forming a new covalent bond and releasing water. Hydrolysis adds water to break a covalent bond, often with enzyme assistance.

**3.** The general formula is (CH2O)n. The n represents the number of carbon atoms, commonly 3-8 for simple carbohydrates.

**4.** Monosaccharides have 1 subunit, e.g. glucose, fructose, ribose. Disaccharides have 2, e.g. sucrose, maltose, lactose. Oligosaccharides have 3-10, e.g. human milk oligosaccharides. Polysaccharides have hundreds or thousands, e.g. starch, glycogen, cellulose, chitin.

**5.** Cellulose is a linear beta-glucose polymer with beta-1,4 glycosidic bonds. Its chains hydrogen-bond with neighbouring hydroxyl groups to form strong microfibrils, making it suitable for plant cell-wall structure. Starch is more readily degraded and mainly used for energy storage.

## Practice Set 2 - A. Short-answer answers

**1.** Lipids are largely non-polar, insoluble in water, dissolve readily in organic solvents, contain mainly C, H and O, and have a smaller proportion of oxygen than carbohydrates. Some may contain P or N.

**2.** Lipids are generally non-polar, so they do not form strong favourable interactions with polar water molecules. Their non-polar regions associate through London dispersion forces and are hydrophobic.

**3.** Saturated fatty acids have no C=C double bonds, so their chains are usually straight and pack closely. Unsaturated fatty acids contain one or more C=C double bonds, creating kinks that prevent close packing.

**4.** A triglyceride forms when glycerol reacts with three fatty acids. Condensation reactions form three ester linkages and release three water molecules.

**5.** Phospholipids are amphipathic: their hydrophilic heads interact with water, while their hydrophobic tails avoid water and associate with each other. This causes bilayers and closed vesicles to form in water.
