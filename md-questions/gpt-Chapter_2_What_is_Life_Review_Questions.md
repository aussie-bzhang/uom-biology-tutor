# Answer Key / 参考答案

## Set 1 answers

### A. Short-answer questions

**1.** Any four: composed of common elements; made of cells; contains genetic information; grows and changes; responds to the environment; uses and makes molecules; extracts and uses energy; exists in populations that can evolve.

**2.** Cells are the fundamental unit of life; all living organisms are composed of cells; all cells come from pre-existing cells; modern cells evolved from a common ancestor. It unifies biology because it applies across living organisms.

**3.** Living organisms share core biochemical features because of common ancestry. Examples include the same genetic code, similar chemical composition, cellular structure, and DNA/RNA/protein-based information and function.

**4.** Individuals vary. Some traits suit the environment better, so those individuals survive and reproduce more successfully. Over many generations, advantageous traits become more common and populations evolve.

**5.** Cyanobacterial photosynthesis increased atmospheric oxygen. Oxygen enabled aerobic respiration, and ozone formation reduced damaging UV radiation, allowing more complex and land-based life to evolve.

## Set 2 answers

### A. Short-answer questions

**1.** Spontaneous origin proposes that life or its precursor molecules formed on early Earth, helped by reducing conditions. Panspermia proposes that life or organic material arrived from elsewhere, such as a comet or meteorite.

**2.** Approximate gaps: prokaryotes to eukaryotes, about 1.4 billion years; eukaryotes to terrestrial/multicellular animal diversification, roughly another 1.5 billion years depending on the landmark used.

**3.** Stromatolites are layered structures associated with microbial communities such as cyanobacteria and resemble very ancient fossil evidence of early life, while some still exist today.

**4.** Prokaryotes: DNA in nucleoid, no membrane-bound organelles, ribosomes present. Eukaryotes: DNA packaged as chromosomes in a nucleus, membrane-bound organelles present, ribosomes present and generally larger.

**5.** Organisms sharing a more recent branch point/common ancestor are more closely related. The tree shows divergence over evolutionary time, not simply “higher” or “lower” organisms.
