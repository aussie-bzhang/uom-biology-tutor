# Answer Key

## Review Set 1 Answers

### A. Short-answer questions

1. Bioenergetics is the study of how organisms manage energy resources. It is essential because all cellular processes, including maintenance, growth, reproduction, transport and biosynthesis, require energy transformations.

2. Potential energy is stored energy, such as chemical energy in glucose or energy in an ion gradient. Kinetic energy is energy of motion, such as muscle movement, molecular motion due to heat, or a falling apple.

3. Organisms exchange both energy and matter with the surroundings: they take in nutrients/light/oxygen and release heat, waste products and CO2. Therefore they are open systems.

4. First law: energy can be transferred and transformed but cannot be created or destroyed. Second law: every energy transfer increases the entropy of the universe; some energy becomes unusable, often as heat.

5. ATP hydrolysis releases free energy. When coupled to an endergonic reaction, often through phosphorylation, the combined process can have a negative net delta G and proceed.

### B. Yes/No questions

1. No; 2. Yes; 3. Yes; 4. No; 5. Yes

### C. Multiple-choice questions

1. B; 2. B; 3. A; 4. B; 5. B; 6. B; 7. A; 8. C; 9. B; 10. B

### D. Applied / calculation questions

1. Net delta G = -7.3 + 4.0 = -3.3 kcal/mol. Because the value is negative, the coupled reaction is exergonic/spontaneous overall.

2. It is exergonic because it releases free energy. It is catabolic because a larger energy-rich molecule is broken down into smaller products.

3. Growth: light energy is converted to chemical energy in sugars. Falling: gravitational potential energy becomes kinetic energy. Digestion/cellular respiration: chemical energy in sugars is transferred to ATP, with some energy lost as heat.

## Review Set 2 Answers

### A. Short-answer questions

1. Biological order can increase locally because organisms are open systems. They import energy and matter and export heat and waste, so total entropy of the universe still increases.

2. ATP can donate a phosphate group to another molecule. This phosphorylation changes the molecule’s energy and/or shape, helping drive chemical, transport or mechanical work.

3. Exergonic reactions have delta G < 0, release free energy and are spontaneous. Endergonic reactions have delta G > 0, require energy input and are nonspontaneous unless coupled to an energy-releasing reaction.

4. Anabolic reactions build larger molecules from smaller ones and usually require energy, e.g. sucrose synthesis. Catabolic reactions break larger molecules into smaller ones and often release energy, e.g. sucrose hydrolysis or cellular respiration.

5. Oxidation is loss of electrons, often associated with gain of oxygen or loss of hydrogen. Reduction is gain of electrons, often associated with loss of oxygen or gain of hydrogen.

### B. Yes/No questions

1. Yes; 2. Yes; 3. No; 4. Yes; 5. No

### C. Multiple-choice questions

1. A; 2. B; 3. B; 4. B; 5. B; 6. C; 7. C; 8. B; 9. A; 10. B

### D. Applied / calculation questions

1. Delta G is negative. The reaction is likely spontaneous because free energy decreases and the system moves toward greater stability.

2. Cells can become more ordered locally, but organisms are open systems. They take in energy and matter and release heat and waste, increasing the total entropy of the universe.

3. Catechol is oxidized because it loses electrons/gains oxygen in the reaction. Oxygen acts as an oxidizing agent because it accepts electrons and is reduced.
