**Lecture 24 Revision Questions**

**Genetic Analysis Involving Multiple Gene Loci / Traits**

*Three practice sets: short-answer, Yes/No, MCQ, and biochemical/application questions*

# Core ideas to revise before attempting the questions

* A dihybrid cross follows two gene loci at the same time, such as Y/y and R/r or B/b and S/s.
* If two loci are on different homologous chromosome pairs, their alleles assort independently during meiosis.
* A heterozygous dihybrid can produce four gamete classes in equal frequency when the loci assort independently.
* A dihybrid x dihybrid cross gives the classic 9:3:3:1 phenotypic ratio when both traits show complete dominance and the loci are independent.
* A dihybrid test cross is especially useful because offspring phenotypes reveal the gametes produced by the dihybrid parent.
* Linked genes are on the same chromosome and do not assort independently; parental gamete classes are usually more frequent than recombinant classes.
* Crossing over during prophase I can generate recombinant chromatids and recombinant gamete classes.
* Deviations from expected Mendelian ratios can indicate linkage, non-Mendelian dominance, gene interaction, sampling error, or viability effects.

# Answer Key

## Practice Set 1 Answers

### A. Short-answer questions

1. A dihybrid cross follows inheritance at two loci at the same time; a monohybrid cross follows one locus.

2. Alleles at different loci on different homologous chromosome pairs separate into gametes independently because each homologous pair aligns independently during meiosis I.

3. YR, Yr, yR and yr.

4. Each trait gives a 3:1 dominant:recessive ratio, and combining two independent traits gives 9 double-dominant, 3 one-dominant-one-recessive, 3 the opposite combination and 1 double-recessive.

5. Linked genes are on the same chromosome, so parental allele combinations tend to be inherited together unless crossing over separates them.

### D. Biochemical and application questions

1. Expected ratio = 9:3:3:1. Yellow round = 9/16 x 1600 = 900; yellow wrinkled = 3/16 x 1600 = 300; green round = 3/16 x 1600 = 300; green wrinkled = 1/16 x 1600 = 100.

2. Yes. A test cross with independent assortment predicts 1:1:1:1. The observed counts are close to 100 in each class, so they are consistent with independent assortment, allowing for sampling variation.

3. Gene Y and gene R affect different biochemical outputs. A y/y genotype disrupts the pigment-related gene product, so colour changes; an r/r genotype disrupts the shape-related gene product, so shape changes. Because the pathways are independent in this model, one mutation does not directly alter the other trait.
