**Meiosis and Allele Segregation**

*Three practice sets: short-answer, Yes/No, MCQ, and biochemical/application questions*

# Core ideas to revise before attempting the questions

* Meiosis is a reductive cell division in sexually reproducing organisms: one diploid cell can produce haploid gametes.
* Meiosis has two divisions: meiosis I separates homologous chromosomes; meiosis II separates sister chromatids.
* Homologous chromosomes pair during prophase I/metaphase I; this does not occur in ordinary mitosis.
* Mendel's Law of Segregation states that the two alleles at a locus separate into different gametes.
* A monohybrid cross can be represented using parental genotypes, gamete genotypes and ratios, a Punnett square, and predicted offspring genotypes/phenotypes.
* For a heterozygote such as Yy, meiosis normally produces 1/2 Y gametes and 1/2 y gametes.
