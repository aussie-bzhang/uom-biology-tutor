## Set 3

### Short-answer questions

1. Constraints include small SA:V ratio, large distance of internal cells to the environment, need for coordination/communication, increased metabolic demand, and greater regulatory complexity.

2. Cells must coordinate movement, feeding, development, metabolism, and homeostasis; specialised cells become dependent on one another.

3. Placozoans have different cell types for sensing, movement, digestion, communication, gravity sensing, and feeding movements, showing division of labour.

4. Larger organisms contain more living biomass and therefore require more energy, oxygen, nutrients, and waste removal capacity.

5. Specialised cells require gene regulation and differentiation at the right time and place, making body construction more complex.
