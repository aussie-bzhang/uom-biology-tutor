**Question 1: Chromosome Analysis in Cell Division**

**a)** Table completion (14 points = 2 points per correct row):

| **Stage** | **Chromosome count** | **Chromatid count** | **Homologous pairs** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| G1 phase | 12 | 12 | 6 |
| Metaphase of mitosis | 12 | 24 | 6 |
| Metaphase I of meiosis | 12 | 24 | 6 |
| Metaphase II of meiosis | 6 | 12 | 0 |
| After meiosis II cytokinesis | 6 | 6 | 0 |

**b)** Explanation (6 points):

• During MITOSIS, homologous chromosomes do NOT pair up or segregate. Sister chromatids separate at anaphase, but each daughter cell receives a complete set of chromosomes (2n = 12), maintaining the diploid number.

• During MEIOSIS, two rounds of division occur. In Meiosis I, homologous chromosomes pair up (synapsis) and separate at Anaphase I, reducing the chromosome number from 2n to n. Meiosis II separates sister chromatids (like mitosis), but starts with haploid cells, so the final products are haploid (n = 6).

• KEY EVENT: The reduction in chromosome number occurs during Anaphase I when homologous chromosomes are pulled to opposite poles. This is the defining feature of meiosis that creates genetic variation through segregation.

**Question 2: Monohybrid Cross and Probability**

**a)**

**(i)** P generation: TT (tall) x tt (dwarf). F1 generation: All Tt (tall). (4 points)

**(ii)** F1 x F1 cross: Tt x Tt. Punnett square yields genotypic ratio 1TT:2Tt:1tt, giving phenotypic ratio 3 tall : 1 dwarf. (4 points)

**b)** Probability calculations (6 points = 2 points each):

• P(homozygous tall) = P(TT) = 1/4 = 0.25 or 25%

• P(homozygous | tall) = P(TT) / P(tall) = (1/4) / (3/4) = 1/3 or 33.3%

• Expected dwarf offspring = 800 x 1/4 = 200. Using binomial: P(X=200) = C(800,200) x (1/4)^200 x (3/4)^600. This is the expected value; the probability of exactly 200 is relatively low, but 200 is the mean of the distribution.

**c)** If the tall F2 plant produces both tall and dwarf offspring in a test cross, its genotype must be Tt (heterozygous). A TT plant would produce only tall offspring (Tt) when crossed with tt. The appearance of dwarf (tt) offspring proves the tall parent contributed a t allele. (6 points)

**Question 3: Dihybrid Cross and Independent Assortment**

**a)**

**(i)** F1 genotype: All AaBb. F1 phenotype: All normal body, normal wing. (2 points)

**(ii)** F2 phenotypic ratio from AaBb x AaBb: 9 normal body/normal wing : 3 normal body/vestigial wing : 3 black body/normal wing : 1 black body/vestigial wing. (6 points)

**b)** Chi-squared analysis:

**(i)** Expected numbers (total 3200): 9/16 x 3200 = 1800 (normal/normal); 3/16 x 3200 = 600 (normal/vestigial); 3/16 x 3200 = 600 (black/normal); 1/16 x 3200 = 200 (black/vestigial). (4 points)

**(ii)** Chi-squared calculation (8 points):

X2 = (1785-1800)^2/1800 + (610-600)^2/600 + (595-600)^2/600 + (210-200)^2/200

X2 = 225/1800 + 100/600 + 25/600 + 100/200

X2 = 0.125 + 0.167 + 0.042 + 0.500 = 0.834

• Since X2 = 0.834 < critical value 7.82, we FAIL TO REJECT the null hypothesis.

• CONCLUSION: The observed results do NOT deviate significantly from the expected 9:3:3:1 ratio. The data support Mendel's law of independent assortment.
