# Key revision focus

* Signal types by source and distribution: autocrine, juxtacrine, paracrine, and endocrine/hormonal signalling.
* Three core stages: signal reception, signal transduction, and cellular response.
* Target-cell specificity: only cells with the right receptor respond to a ligand.
* Membrane receptors versus intracellular receptors: relation to ligand size, polarity, and membrane permeability.
* Major receptor types: ligand-gated ion channels, enzyme-coupled receptors, and G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs).
* Signal amplification, integration, branching, and short-term versus long-term responses.
* Plant cell signalling: guard cells, stomata, CO2, humidity, light, and hormones.

# Brief study notes

* A cell responds to a signal only if it has the appropriate receptor and intracellular response pathway.
* Large or polar ligands usually use cell-surface receptors; small nonpolar ligands can often use intracellular receptors.
* GPCR pathways are powerful because they use GTP/GDP switching, second messengers such as cAMP, and kinase cascades.
* Signal transduction is not merely a wire: it can relay, amplify, integrate, and branch signals.
* In plants, guard-cell signalling connects environmental conditions to stomatal opening or closure.
