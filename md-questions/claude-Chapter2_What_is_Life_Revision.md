## Revision Set 1 — Answers

### A. Short answer

**1.** Cell theory: (i) cells are the fundamental unit of life; (ii) all living organisms are made of cells; (iii) all cells come from pre-existing cells; (iv) modern cells evolved from a common ancestor.

**2.** Any five of: composed of a common set of elements; comprised of cells; contain genetic information; grow and change; respond to the environment; use and make molecules; extract and use energy; exist in populations and can evolve.

**3.** “Biochemical unity of life” means that because all organisms are related, they share the same genetic code, similar chemical composition and similar cellular structure. This is strong evidence that all life on Earth arose from a single common ancestor (~4 billion years ago).

**4.** (1) Spontaneous formation on early Earth — a reducing environment allowed organic molecules (e.g. DNA bases) to form; evidence = Miller–Urey experiments. (2) Extra-terrestrial origin / panspermia — life or its building blocks arrived from another planet or comet; evidence = the Murchison meteorite.

**5.** (a) Earth formed ~4.5 billion years ago (bya); (b) prokaryotes ~3.5 bya; (c) eukaryotes ~2.1 bya. Gap between prokaryotes and eukaryotes ≈ 1.4 billion years.

### B. Yes / No

**1.** No — all cells come from pre-existing cells.

**2.** Yes.

**3.** Yes — unicellular eukaryotes (~2.1 bya) appeared long before multicellular animals (~600 mya).

**4.** No — a virus is not a cell and is not covered by cell theory.

**5.** Yes.

**.** 1-B, 2-C, 3-B, 4-C, 5-A, 6-B, 7-C, 8-D, 9-B, 10-B

### D. Applied / experiment

**1.** (a) Reducing (not oxidising). A reducing atmosphere lacks free O2, which would otherwise break down/oxidise newly formed organic molecules; this allows organic molecules to accumulate. (b) Examples include amino acids and DNA/RNA bases (nucleobases).

**2.** (a) 3.5 − 2.1 = 1.4 billion years. (b) 2.1 bya − 0.6 bya (600 mya) = 1.5 billion years.

## Revision Set 2 — Answers

### A. Short answer

**1.** (1) Variation: individuals in a species show a wide range of inherited variation. (2) Differential reproduction: individuals whose characteristics are best suited to the environment (the “fitter” individuals) are more likely to survive and reproduce. (3) Evolution: given enough time, the accumulation of these advantageous traits changes the population and the species may evolve into a new form.

**2.** 6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2. It releases O2, which (i) made aerobic cellular respiration possible and (ii) led to the formation of the ozone layer — both of which created the conditions for the evolution of complex and terrestrial life.

**3.** Cyanobacteria photosynthesised, converting CO2 into glucose and O2. UV light split O2 into oxygen radicals; these radicals reacted with O2 molecules to form ozone (O3); ozone molecules accumulated to form a layer in the upper atmosphere.

**4.** (1) Alteration of the atmosphere, allowing aerobic cellular respiration. (2) Formation of the ozone layer, which blocks UV-C and partially UV-A/UV-B, creating the conditions for the evolution of life on land.

**5.** The three domains are Bacteria, Archaea and Eukarya. Bacteria and Archaea are prokaryotic. Archaea are most closely related to Eukarya.

### B. Yes / No

**1.** Yes.

**2.** No — Archaea are more closely related to Eukarya.

**3.** Yes.

**4.** No — oxygen had to be produced by cyanobacteria first.

**5.** Yes (and it partially absorbs UV-A and UV-B).

### D. Applied / calculation

**1.** (a) Left side: 6 CO2 = 12 O atoms, plus 6 H2O = 6 O atoms → 18 O atoms. Right side: C6H12O6 = 6 O atoms, plus 6 O2 = 12 O atoms → 18 O atoms. Both sides = 18, so balanced. (b) 12 CO2 is double the equation, so 2 molecules of glucose and 12 molecules of O2 are produced.

**2.** Photosynthesis releases O2 → UV light splits O2 into two oxygen radicals (O) → each radical reacts with an O2 molecule (O + O2 → O3) → ozone molecules accumulate and congregate to form the ozone layer in the stratosphere.

## Revision Set 3 — Answers

### A. Short answer

**1.** All prokaryotic cells have: a cell (plasma) membrane; DNA in a region called the nucleoid; cytoplasm; and ribosomes.

**2.** Any three, e.g.: prokaryotes have no membrane-bound organelles (eukaryotes do); prokaryotic DNA is free in the cytoplasm as a nucleoid whereas eukaryotic DNA is packaged as chromosomes in a nucleus; prokaryotic cell walls are peptidoglycan (except Archaea) whereas eukaryotic walls (where present) contain cellulose/chitin etc.; prokaryotes have 70S ribosomes vs 80S in eukaryotes; prokaryotes are generally smaller.

**3.** Prokaryotic ribosome: 70S = 50S (large) + 30S (small), ~55 proteins and 3 rRNA, ~2.3 MDa. Eukaryotic ribosome: 80S = 60S (large) + 40S (small), ~80 proteins and 4 rRNA, ~3.3 MDa.

**4.** Plantae — multicellular, autotroph (cell wall of cellulose). Protista — unicellular, autotroph or heterotroph. Animalia — multicellular, heterotroph (no cell wall). Fungi — multicellular, heterotroph (cell wall of chitin).

**5.** A phylogenetic tree shows the evolutionary relationships and divergence between groups from common ancestors. The horizontal axis usually represents time (e.g. millions of years ago, from past on the left to present on the right).

### B. Yes / No

**1.** No.

**2.** Yes.

**3.** No — plant cell walls are made of cellulose; peptidoglycan is the bacterial cell wall.

**4.** Yes.

**5.** Yes.

### D. Applied

**1.** Svedberg (S) units measure how fast a particle sediments in a centrifuge. The sedimentation rate depends on a particle’s mass, size AND shape (not mass alone), so it is not additive. When the 50S and 30S subunits combine, the resulting 70S particle has a different overall size and shape, giving a sedimentation value (70S) that is less than the simple sum (80S).

**2.** Bacterial (70S) ribosomes differ structurally from human (80S) ribosomes — different rRNAs, proteins, size and shape. Antibiotics that target the 70S ribosome bind to features unique to bacteria, blocking bacterial protein synthesis, while the structurally different 80S human ribosome is not bound — so bacterial cells are killed/inhibited but human cells are largely unaffected (selective toxicity).
