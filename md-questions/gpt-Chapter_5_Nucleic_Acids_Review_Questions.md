# Answer Key

## Set A Answers

### A1. Short-answer answers

1. A nucleotide contains a nitrogenous base, a pentose sugar and a phosphate group. A nucleoside contains only a base plus a sugar; adding phosphate makes it a nucleotide.

2. DNA is usually double-stranded, contains deoxyribose, and uses A, C, G and T. RNA is usually single-stranded, contains ribose, and uses A, C, G and U.

3. The sugar-phosphate backbone is the repeating sugar and phosphate chain of a nucleic acid strand. Nucleotides are linked by phosphodiester bonds formed through condensation reactions.

4. The central dogma describes information flow from DNA to RNA by transcription, then from RNA to polypeptide by translation. In most cases, information in polypeptides is not converted back into nucleic acid sequence.

5. mRNA carries a transcribed gene sequence; tRNA brings amino acids and matches anticodons with mRNA codons; rRNA forms a structural and functional part of ribosomes.

### A2. Yes/No answers

1. Yes

2. Yes

3. No - phosphodiester bonds link nucleotides within a strand; hydrogen bonds occur between bases on opposite strands.

4. Yes

5. No - transcription signals are different from translation start/stop codons.

### A4. Sequence/application answers

1. mRNA: 5'-AUG GCU UAA-3'. AUG starts translation; UAA is a stop codon, so translation stops at UAA after Met-Ala.

2. Codons: AUG | GCU | UUU | UGA. Amino-acid sequence: Met-Ala-Phe, then stop at UGA.

## Set B Answers

### B1. Short-answer answers

1. DNA normally has two complementary antiparallel strands. RNA is usually one strand, but complementary regions within the same RNA can hydrogen bond and fold into three-dimensional structures.

2. A gene is a DNA sequence that encodes a functional product, commonly a polypeptide. Coding regions are translated into protein sequence, while non-coding regions may be transcribed but not translated, or may regulate transcription such as promoters.

3. A codon is a three-base unit in mRNA. AUG is the start codon and codes for methionine. UAA, UAG and UGA are stop codons. An ORF is a continuous set of non-overlapping codons from start to stop.

4. Each DNA strand can be read in three possible frames depending on which nucleotide begins the first codon. Because there are two strands, this gives 3 forward and 3 reverse frames, or six total.

5. Transcription copies nucleotide information from DNA into RNA. Translation decodes mRNA codons into an amino-acid sequence, producing a polypeptide.

### B2. Yes/No answers

1. Yes

2. No - double-stranded DNA can have six possible reading frames.

3. Yes

4. No - stop codons signal termination and do not add an amino acid.

5. Yes
