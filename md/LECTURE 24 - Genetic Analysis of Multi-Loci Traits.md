Week 8, Lecture 24
Genetic analysis involving multiple gene loci/traits
A/Prof Hayley Bugeja
CRICOS

Learning outcomes
By the end of this lecture, you will be able to relate the independent assortment of
alleles for genes on different homologous chromosome pairs to the observed
outcomes of a dihybrid cross.
This includes being able to:
• Diagram the ways that alleles on different homologous chromosome pairs can be
segregated during meiosis in a dihybrid cross.
• Use Mendel's law of independent assortment to explain and calculate genotypic and
phenotypic ratios across multiple loci.
• Recognise that alleles of different gene loci that are on the same chromosome may
not assort independently of each other.
