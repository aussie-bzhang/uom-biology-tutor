Cell Cycle and DNA replication

For cell division to be successful, the genetic
material in the nucleus must be duplicated
accurately, and one copy must be distributed to
each daughter cell. This duplication of the cell’s
genetic material is called DNA replication.

Summary

• Cytoplasm is made up of the cytosol, cytoskeleton and organelles.

• Cytoskeleton has three types; microfilaments, intermediate filaments and microtubules.

• DNA replication is semi-conservative and involves adding nucleotides in a 5’ →3’

direction.

• DNA replication differs in prokaryotes to eukaryotes largely based on number of origins

of replication.

• The cell cycle in eukaryotes is regulated by proteins – cyclins and cyclin dependent

kinases.

• Mitosis is the creation of genetically identical nuclei in eukaryotes. Binary fission is the

division of a prokaryotic cell.
