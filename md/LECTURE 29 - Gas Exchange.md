Enhancing diffusion maximises gas exchange
Properties of the lung that maximise diffusion:
• Large surface areas
• Maximise the partial pressure gradient
• Minimise the diffusion that takes place in an
aqueous medium
• Thin barrier (minimise diffusion distance)

area of a tennis court! (~85m2)

Structure of sponges
Adult sponges have simple organisation:
• Skeleton made of collagen (protein) and
spicules (siliceous or calcareous)
• Water channels
• Water current moved through the
channels to bring in food and move
respiratory gases.
Page [27]

Cell types in sponges
• Different cell types including
choanocytes (collar cells).
• Collar cells have flagella that beat to
create the water current through
sponges.
• Collar cells very similar in structure to
unicellular and colonial protists call
choanoflagellates.
• Morphological similarity supports the
theory that unicellular protists >>
