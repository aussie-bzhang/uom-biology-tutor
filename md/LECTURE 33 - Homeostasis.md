Communication with hormones – Recap

Communication with hormones (chemical communication)
• Slower (than with electrical signals) but longer lasting

Plant hormones
• Respond to various stimuli (external and internal)
• Light, day length, temperature, gravity, nutrients, water, developmental
• Six types/classes (one of which is abscisic acid, example for today)

Animal hormones
• Hydrophilic (water soluble) or lipophilic (lipid soluble)
• Transported through blood
• Act on either extracellular receptors (if hydrophilic) or receptors inside cell (if lipophilic)
