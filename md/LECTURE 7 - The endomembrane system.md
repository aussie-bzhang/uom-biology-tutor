The Endomembrane system

A system of compartments that includes:

- All of the membrane-bound components of the cell

(including the nuclear envelope).

- Does not include the mitochondria, chloroplasts and

microbodies.

We will focus on;

- Endoplasmic Reticulum

- Golgi Apparatus

- Lysosomes and Plant Vacuoles

Endocytosis: Packing materials into a vesicle for
transport into a cell.

Exocytosis: Releasing materials and waste out of a cell.

Golgi apparatus

• Collects, packages, and distributes molecules

• Consists of flattened stacks of membrane or cisternae
called Golgi stacks. Collectively, all the Golgi stacks in a
cell are called the Golgi apparatus

• Golgi stacks are functional extensions of the ER and

constantly receive vesicles from the ER.

• Golgi have two faces. Vesicles arrive at the cis face
(receiving) and leave at the trans face (shipping).

Lysosomes

• Recycle bins of the animal cell, surrounded

by single membrane.

• Break down material ingested by endocytosis

or recycle old organelles (autophagy).

• Acidic interior and approximately 40 different

hydrolytic enzymes derived from the rough

ER and Golgi.

•

•

Lysosomes have an internal pH of 4.5

They can also be used to digest pathogens

that enter the cell

Plant vacuoles

• The plant equivalent of lysosomes,

surrounded by a single membrane

called the tonoplast.

• Contain hydrolytic enzymes and

serve as degradative compartments.

• Vacuoles also perform a diverse

range of other functions – storage of

nutrients, pigments and maintenance

of cell turgor pressure.

Summary

• The plasma membrane is fundamental for transport of substances in and out of a cell.

•

 It contains phospholipids (saturated and unsaturated), cholesterol, and membrane proteins.

• Saturation of phospholipid fatty acid tails leads to closer packing, and reduced permeability.

• Cholesterol enhances packing of fatty acid tails at most temperatures but disrupts it at low

temperatures.

• Passive transport (diffusion) is the normal movement of a substance with its concentration

gradient.

• Active transport requires energy, and goes against an electrochemical gradient.

• The endomembrane system is an extension of the plasma membrane, that makes, packages and

distributes proteins, lipids and carbohydrates.
