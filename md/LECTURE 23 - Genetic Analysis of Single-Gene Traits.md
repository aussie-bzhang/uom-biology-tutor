Week 8, Lecture 23
Genetic analysis involving single gene traits
A/Prof Hayley Bugeja
CRICOS

Learning outcomes
By the end of this lecture, you will be able to use genetic diagrams and
nomenclature to illustrate how traits are inherited and predict the outcomes of a
genetic cross.
This includes being able to:
• Describe the utility of different types of genetic crosses (test cross, back cross,
reciprocal cross) in observing inheritance patterns.
• Distinguish between the transmission of traits carried on autosomes and the X-
chromosome.
• Demonstrate how information can be assembled into pedigrees and interpreted in
order to describe how traits are inherited in humans.
