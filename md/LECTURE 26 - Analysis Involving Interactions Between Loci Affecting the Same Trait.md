Learning outcomes
By the end of this lecture, you will be able to predict the outcomes of a genetic cross
where multiple genes contribute to the same phenotype.
This includes being able to:
• Describe how a single trait can be influenced by more than one gene.
• Explain how different gene interactions will influence the phenotypes observed.

Think- pair- share: Influence of epistasis on coat colour
There are several genes determining coat colour in dogs and cats.
The black/brown locus (B/b) determines pigment colour
- Black is dominant to brown
The expressed/not-expressed (E/e) locus determines if colour is expressed or not.
- The phenotype where colour is expressed is dominant to not-expressed which results in a cream
coat colour.
