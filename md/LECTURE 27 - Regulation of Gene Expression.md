Learning outcomes
By the end of this lecture, you will be able to explain the different ways that gene
expression can be controlled and why this may be useful.
This includes being able to:
• Recognise the importance of being able to control the amount of gene product
temporally, spatially or in response to environmental signals.
• Identify the different points during the expression of a gene that can be controlled to
influence the amount of gene product.
• Interpret data relating to simple models of positive and negative gene regulation.
