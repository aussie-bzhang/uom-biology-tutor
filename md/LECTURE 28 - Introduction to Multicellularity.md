Topic 4 Learning Outcomes
At the end of Topic 4, you should be able to:
• Describe the benefits and consequences of multicellularity.
• Describe how animal circulatory systems function to transport nutrients and waste
throughout the body.
• Explain how the transport needs of plants are met by the vascular tissue.
• Describe mechanisms for cell-to-cell communication in a complex multicellular organism
(both chemical & electrical).
• Compare and contrast how multicellular animals and plants develop.
• Describe multicellular organisms as a hierarchy of specialised cell types forming distinct
tissues which form functional organs.

The transition to multicellularity
Probably occurred in several steps:
• Aggregation of cells into a cluster
• Intercellular communication within the cluster
Transition to multicellularity also
• Specialisation of some cells within the cluster (cooperation)  results in individual cells losing
the ability to live independently
• Organisation of specialised cells into groups (tissues)
