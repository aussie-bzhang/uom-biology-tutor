Learning outcomes
By the end of this lecture, you will be able to relate the process of meiosis to the
segregation of genetic information during sexual reproduction.
This includes being able to:
• Describe the stages of meiosis and contrast this with mitosis.
• Diagram chromosomes to show how alleles segregrate at a gene locus during
meiosis.
• Use Mendel's first law of allelic segregation to explain observed inheritance
patterns.
• Diagram all components of a simple monohybrid cross and complete a
Punnett square to predict outcomes.

Comparison of meiosis with mitosis
• Meiosis involves two cell division events – meiosis I and meiosis II
• Key difference include:
‒ Chromosomes pair (synapse) during prophase I & metaphase I in meiosis but not
during mitosis
‒ Sister chromatids separate at the end of mitosis & at the end of meiosis II
‒ Homologous chromosomes separate at the end of meiosis I
‒ Meiosis is a reductive division. Reducing the number of chromosomes by 1/2
