# Anaerobic Respiration

## Overview
Anaerobic respiration is a process used by some prokaryotic organisms in the absence of oxygen. It involves an electron transport chain that uses alternative electron acceptors, such as sulfate or nitrate, instead of oxygen.

## Key points
- Anaerobic respiration occurs in the absence of oxygen
- It uses alternative electron acceptors
- It is less efficient than aerobic respiration
- Examples include sulfate-reducing bacteria
