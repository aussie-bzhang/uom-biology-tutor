# Genetic Maps and Map Distance

## Overview
Genetic maps are constructed by analyzing the frequency of recombination between linked genes. These maps provide a visual representation of the relative positions of genes on a chromosome. Map distance, calculated from recombination frequency, is used to predict the likelihood of recombination events and the outcomes of genetic crosses.

## Key points
- Genetic maps are based on the frequency of recombination between linked genes.
- Map distance is a measure of the physical distance between genes on a chromosome.
- Map distance can be used to predict the outcomes of genetic crosses.
