# Specialized Exchange Surfaces

## Overview
Specialized exchange surfaces, such as the alveoli in the lungs and the spongy mesophyll cells in plants, have large surface areas and thin barriers, maximizing the efficiency of gas exchange. These surfaces are designed to facilitate the diffusion of gases and nutrients across the membrane.

## Key points
- Large surface areas maximize gas exchange
- Thin barriers minimize diffusion distance
- Specialized exchange surfaces are designed for efficient gas exchange
