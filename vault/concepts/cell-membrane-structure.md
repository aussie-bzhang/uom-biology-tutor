# Cell Membrane Structure

## Overview
The plasma membrane is composed of a phospholipid bilayer with hydrophobic tails facing inward and hydrophilic heads facing outward. This structure prevents the free flow of liquids into/out of cells. The fluidity of the membrane is maintained by the movement of phospholipids and the presence of cholesterol, which affects permeability at different temperatures.

## Key points
- The plasma membrane is a phospholipid bilayer with hydrophobic tails and hydrophilic heads.
- The presence of cholesterol in the membrane affects its permeability at various temperatures.
- The fluidity of the membrane is crucial for its proper functioning.
