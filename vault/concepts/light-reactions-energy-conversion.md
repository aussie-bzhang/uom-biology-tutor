# Conversion of Light Energy to Chemical Energy in Light Reactions

## Overview
The light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis convert light energy into chemical energy in the form of ATP and NADPH. This process involves the absorption of light by chlorophyll, the splitting of water molecules, and the generation of ATP and NADPH.

## Key points
- Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll
- Water molecules are split during the process
- ATP and NADPH are produced as chemical energy carriers
