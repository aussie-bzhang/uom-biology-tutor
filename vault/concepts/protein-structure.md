# Protein Structure: Primary, Secondary, Tertiary

## Overview
Proteins are complex molecules with a hierarchical structure. The primary structure is the linear sequence of amino acids, the secondary structure is the local folding patterns, the tertiary structure is the overall three-dimensional shape, and the quaternary structure involves multiple polypeptide chains. Interactions like hydrogen bonding, disulfide bonds, and hydrophobic interactions play crucial roles in maintaining protein structure.

## Key points
- Protein structure is organized into primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary levels.
- Hydrogen bonding, disulfide bonds, and hydrophobic interactions are key forces that stabilize protein structure.
