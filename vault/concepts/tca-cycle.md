# Tricarboxylic Acid (TCA) Cycle

## Overview
The TCA cycle, also known as the citric acid cycle or Krebs cycle, is a series of chemical reactions that occur in the mitochondria. It oxidizes acetyl CoA to carbon dioxide, generating energy in the form of ATP, NADH, and FADH2. The cycle is crucial for the production of ATP and the generation of precursors for anabolic pathways.

## Key points
- The TCA cycle occurs in the mitochondria and is a key part of cellular respiration.
- It generates ATP and high-energy electron carriers (NADH and FADH2).
- The cycle provides precursors for anabolic reactions.
