# Signal Transduction Pathways

## Overview
Signal transduction pathways are complex networks of interactions that relay signals from the cell surface to the nucleus. They can amplify signals, integrate multiple signals, and distribute signals to multiple effector proteins, leading to a variety of cellular responses.

## Key points
- Signal transduction pathways can relay signals, amplify them, integrate multiple signals, and distribute signals to multiple effectors.
- These pathways are essential for coordinating cellular responses to external signals.
