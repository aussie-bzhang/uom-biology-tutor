# Plant Growth and Meristems

## Overview
Plant growth is driven by the activity of meristems, which are regions of undifferentiated cells capable of continuous division. Primary growth involves the elongation of stems and roots, while secondary growth involves the thickening of stems and branches. Meristems are essential for the continuous growth and development of plants.

## Key points
- Primary growth is characterized by the elongation of stems and roots, driven by apical meristems.
- Secondary growth is characterized by the thickening of stems and branches, driven by lateral meristems.
- Meristems are regions of undifferentiated cells that can divide indefinitely, contributing to the continuous growth of plants.
