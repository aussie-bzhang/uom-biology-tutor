# ATP Structure and Function

## Overview
ATP (adenosine triphosphate) is a nucleotide that serves as the primary energy currency in cells. It consists of an adenine base, a ribose sugar, and three phosphate groups. The hydrolysis of ATP releases energy, which is used to drive endergonic reactions in the cell.

## Key points
- ATP has a complex structure with an adenine base, ribose sugar, and three phosphate groups
- ATP hydrolysis releases energy for cellular processes
