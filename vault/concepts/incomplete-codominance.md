# Incomplete and Codominance

## Overview
Incomplete dominance occurs when the heterozygous phenotype is intermediate between the two homozygous phenotypes. Codominance happens when both alleles contribute to the phenotype, resulting in a mixed or distinct phenotype. These concepts expand upon the basic principles of dominance and recessivity, providing a more nuanced understanding of inheritance patterns.

## Key points
- Incomplete dominance results in an intermediate phenotype.
- Codominance leads to a mixed or distinct phenotype.
- These concepts provide a more nuanced understanding of inheritance patterns beyond simple dominance and recessivity.
