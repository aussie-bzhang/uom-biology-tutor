# Nucleus Structure

## Overview
The nucleus is a double-membrane-bound organelle containing the cell's DNA, histones, and nuclear pores. It is the site of DNA replication and gene transcription. The nucleus likely evolved from the invagination of the plasma membrane around the nucleoid of an ancient prokaryote.

## Key points
- The nucleus is surrounded by a double membrane with nuclear pores for molecule transport
- DNA is organized into chromosomes within the nucleus
- The nucleus is continuous with the endoplasmic reticulum
- The nucleus evolved through membrane invagination
