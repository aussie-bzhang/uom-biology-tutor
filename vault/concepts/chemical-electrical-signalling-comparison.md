# Comparison of Chemical and Electrical Signalling

## Overview
Chemical and electrical signalling are two fundamental modes of communication in multicellular organisms. Chemical signals, such as hormones, are involved in long-distance communication and can be paracrine, autocrine, or hormonal. Electrical signals, like action potentials, are rapid and involve the propagation of electrical impulses along neurons. Both types of signals are essential for coordinating cellular activities and maintaining homeostasis.

## Key points
- Chemical and electrical signals are two primary modes of cell communication.
- Chemical signals are involved in long-distance communication, while electrical signals are rapid and propagate along neurons.
