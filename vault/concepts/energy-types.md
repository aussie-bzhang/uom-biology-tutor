# Types of Energy in Biology

## Overview
Biological systems utilize two primary types of energy: potential energy, which is stored energy, and kinetic energy, which is energy of motion. Potential energy can be stored in various forms, such as in chemical bonds or concentration gradients, while kinetic energy is associated with the movement of molecules.

## Key points
- Potential energy is stored energy, often in the form of chemical bonds or concentration gradients
- Kinetic energy is the energy of motion, associated with the movement of molecules
