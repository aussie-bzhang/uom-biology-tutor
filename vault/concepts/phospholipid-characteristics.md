# Phospholipid Characteristics

## Overview
Phospholipids have a hydrophobic fatty acid tail and a hydrophilic phosphate head. The hydrophobic tails are repelled by water and are attracted to each other, while the hydrophilic heads interact with water. This unique structure allows phospholipids to form bilayers in water, which is essential for the structure of cell membranes.

## Key points
- Phospholipids have a hydrophobic tail and a hydrophilic head.
- The hydrophobic tails of phospholipids are repelled by water and are attracted to each other.
- The hydrophilic heads of phospholipids interact with water.
