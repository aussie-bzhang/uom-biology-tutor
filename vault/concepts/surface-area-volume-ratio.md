# Surface Area to Volume Ratio

## Overview
The surface area to volume ratio is a critical factor in multicellular organisms, as it determines the efficiency of diffusion. Smaller organisms have a larger surface area to volume ratio, allowing for more efficient gas exchange and nutrient distribution. As organisms grow larger, this ratio decreases, leading to limitations in diffusion and necessitating specialized gas exchange structures.

## Key points
- Smaller organisms have a larger surface area to volume ratio
- Larger organisms have a smaller surface area to volume ratio
- This ratio affects the efficiency of diffusion in multicellular organisms
