# Protein Denaturation: Causes and Effects

## Overview
Protein denaturation refers to the disruption of the secondary or tertiary structure of a protein, leading to a loss of function. Factors such as high temperature, pH changes, and the presence of polar or non-polar molecules can cause denaturation by disrupting the intermolecular forces that maintain protein structure.

## Key points
- Denaturation is the alteration of a protein's secondary or tertiary structure, resulting in a loss of function.
- Conditions that can cause denaturation include high temperature, pH changes, and the presence of polar or non-polar molecules.
