# Water Transport in Plants

## Overview
Water moves through plants via the xylem, driven by water potential and transpiration. Water potential determines the direction of water movement, and transpiration creates a pull that moves water from roots to leaves.

## Key points
- Water moves through the xylem from roots to leaves.
- Water potential drives water movement across selectively permeable membranes.
- Transpiration creates a pull that moves water upwards in the xylem.
