# C3, C4, and CAM Photosynthesis

## Overview
C3, C4, and CAM are different photosynthetic pathways that plants have evolved to optimize carbon fixation under various environmental conditions. C3 plants use the standard Calvin cycle, C4 plants use a modified Calvin cycle with an initial CO2 fixation step in mesophyll cells, and CAM plants fix CO2 at night and store it as malate during the day.

## Key points
- C3 plants use the standard Calvin cycle and are efficient in moderate climates
- C4 plants have a more efficient CO2 fixation process and are adapted to hot and dry environments
- CAM plants conserve water by opening stomata at night and are found in arid regions
