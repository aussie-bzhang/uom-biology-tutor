# Positive Feedback Loop

## Overview
A positive feedback loop is a regulatory mechanism that amplifies a process, often leading to a rapid and sometimes excessive response. It is less common in homeostasis regulation but can be critical in certain physiological processes, such as blood clotting and childbirth. Positive feedback loops do not maintain stability but rather drive a process to completion.

## Key points
- Positive feedback loops amplify a process, often leading to a rapid and sometimes excessive response.
- They are less common in homeostasis regulation but are critical in specific physiological processes.
- Examples include blood clotting and childbirth.
