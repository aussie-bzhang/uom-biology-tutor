# Action Potential Generation and Propagation

## Overview
Action potentials are the electrical signals generated by neurons. They are initiated by a change in membrane potential due to the influx of sodium ions, followed by the efflux of potassium ions. This process involves depolarization, repolarization, and hyperpolarization, and is essential for the transmission of signals along the neuron. The action potential is propagated along the axon, facilitated by myelination and the Nodes of Ranvier.

## Key points
- Action potentials are the electrical signals generated by neurons for signal transmission.
- The process involves depolarization, repolarization, and hyperpolarization.
- Myelination and Nodes of Ranvier enhance the speed of action potential propagation.
