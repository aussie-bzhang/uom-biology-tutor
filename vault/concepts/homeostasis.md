# Homeostasis

## Overview
Homeostasis is the maintenance of a stable internal environment in multicellular organisms. It relies on feedback mechanisms, which can be either positive or negative, to regulate physiological processes. Negative feedback loops are crucial for maintaining homeostasis by counteracting deviations from a set point, while positive feedback loops amplify a process. In plants, cell-cell communication is essential for water homeostasis, with hormones like abscisic acid playing a key role in responding to water stress.

## Key points
- Homeostasis is the maintenance of a steady state within an organism.
- Negative feedback loops are used to counteract deviations from a set point, while positive feedback loops amplify a process.
- Cells communicate to maintain water homeostasis in plants, with hormones like abscisic acid mediating responses to water stress.
