# Prokaryotic vs. Eukaryotic Cells

## Overview
Prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells differ in the presence of membrane-bound organelles, cell wall composition, DNA packaging, and ribosome structure. Prokaryotes lack membrane-bound organelles and have simpler structures, while eukaryotes have more complex organelles and structures.

## Key points
- Prokaryotic cells lack membrane-bound organelles
- Eukaryotic cells have membrane-bound organelles
- Prokaryotic cell walls are made of peptidoglycan
- Eukaryotic cell walls vary by kingdom
- Prokaryotic DNA is free in the cytoplasm
- Eukaryotic DNA is packaged into chromosomes
- Prokaryotic ribosomes are 70S
- Eukaryotic ribosomes are 80S
