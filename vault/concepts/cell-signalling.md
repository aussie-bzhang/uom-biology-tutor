# Cell Signalling

## Overview
Cell signalling is the process by which cells communicate with each other. It involves the detection of chemical signals and the transduction of these signals into cellular responses. Specificity in signal transduction is achieved through the interaction of ligands with specific receptors, and different cells respond to signals differently due to variations in receptor expression and intracellular pathways.

## Key points
- Cells communicate through various types of signals, including autocrine, juxtacrine, paracrine, and hormonal signals.
- Signal detection occurs via membrane receptors for large or polar ligands and intracellular receptors for small or nonpolar ligands.
- Receptors can be ion channels, enzyme-coupled, or G protein-coupled, each with distinct mechanisms of signal transduction.
