# Irreversible Inhibition

## Overview
Irreversible inhibition involves the covalent bonding of an inhibitor to the active site of an enzyme, permanently inactivating it. This type of inhibition is often seen in the action of toxins and certain drugs.

## Key points
- Irreversible inhibitors form a covalent bond with the enzyme, permanently inactivating it
- Examples of irreversible inhibitors include toxins and certain antibiotics
- Irreversible inhibition is not reversible
