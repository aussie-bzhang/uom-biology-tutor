# Cell Potency in Animal Development

## Overview
Cell potency refers to the ability of cells to differentiate into various cell types. In animal development, cells differ in their potency, with totipotent cells capable of forming the entire organism, pluripotent cells forming most body cell types but not extraembryonic tissues, and unipotent cells forming only one type of cell. Understanding cell potency is crucial for understanding the early stages of development.

## Key points
- Totipotent cells can produce all cell types of an organism.
- Pluripotent cells can produce all body cell types but not extraembryonic tissues.
- Multipotent cells can produce several cell types.
- Unipotent cells can produce only one cell type.
