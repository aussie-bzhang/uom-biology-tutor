# Metabolic Pathways

## Overview
Metabolic pathways are a series of biochemical reactions that occur in living systems. They are categorized into catabolism, which involves the breakdown of complex molecules into simpler ones, and anabolism, which involves the synthesis of complex molecules from simpler ones. Carbohydrates, lipids, and proteins are key molecules in these pathways, providing energy and building blocks for cellular processes.

## Key points
- Catabolism breaks down molecules into simpler forms, releasing energy, while anabolism builds complex molecules from simpler ones, requiring energy
- Carbohydrates, lipids, and proteins are central to energy production and cellular structure
- Metabolic pathways are interconnected, with products of one pathway often serving as substrates for another
