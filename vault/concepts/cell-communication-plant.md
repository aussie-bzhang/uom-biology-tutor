# Cell-Cell Communication in Plants

## Overview
Plants have evolved complex mechanisms for cell-cell communication, which are essential for maintaining water homeostasis. Hormones like abscisic acid play a key role in responding to water stress by closing stomata and reducing transpiration. This communication involves signal transduction pathways and the efflux of ions and water from guard cells.

## Key points
- Plants use cell-cell communication to maintain water homeostasis.
- Hormones like abscisic acid are involved in responding to water stress.
- Communication involves signal transduction pathways and ion efflux from guard cells.
