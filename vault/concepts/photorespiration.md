# Photorespiration in Plants

## Overview
Photorespiration is a process that occurs in plants when RuBP is incorrectly fixed by oxygen instead of CO2, leading to the production of phosphoglycolate. This process is inefficient and can reduce plant productivity. It is more likely to occur in C3 plants under conditions of high oxygen and low CO2, such as on hot, dry days.

## Key points
- Photorespiration is an inefficient process that occurs when RuBP is fixed by oxygen
- It is more common in C3 plants and can reduce plant productivity
- High oxygen and low CO2 concentrations can trigger photorespiration
