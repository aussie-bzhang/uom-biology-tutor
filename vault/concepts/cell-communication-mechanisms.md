# Cell-to-Cell Communication Mechanisms

## Overview
Cell-to-cell communication in multicellular organisms is essential for coordination and homeostasis. This communication can occur through chemical signals, such as hormones, and electrical signals, like action potentials. Chemical signals can be autocrine, juxtacrine, paracrine, or hormonal, each with distinct modes of action and target cells. Electrical signals are generated by neurons and involve the propagation of action potentials, which are essential for rapid communication within the nervous system.

## Key points
- Cell-to-cell communication is vital for multicellular organisms to maintain homeostasis and coordinate functions.
- Chemical signals can be autocrine, juxtacrine, paracrine, or hormonal, affecting different types of cells.
- Electrical signals are generated by neurons and involve the propagation of action potentials.
