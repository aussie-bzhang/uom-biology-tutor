# Gas Exchange in Sponges

## Overview
Sponges, simple aquatic animals, use their body cavity and collar cells to exchange gases. The water current created by the flagella of collar cells brings in food and moves respiratory gases, facilitating gas exchange. The structure of sponges and the movement of water current are related to Fick's law, as they maximize surface area and minimize diffusion distance.

## Key points
- Sponges use their body cavity and collar cells for gas exchange
- Water current created by collar cells facilitates gas exchange
- Structure of sponges and water current are related to Fick's law
