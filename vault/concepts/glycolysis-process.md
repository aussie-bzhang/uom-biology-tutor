# Glycolysis Process

## Overview
Glycolysis is a series of ten enzyme-catalyzed steps that occur in the cytoplasm of cells, converting glucose into two molecules of pyruvate. It produces two ATP and two NADH, and is an essential step in cellular respiration. The process involves energy investment in the first five steps and energy harvesting in the last five steps.

## Key points
- Glycolysis occurs in the cytoplasm and converts glucose to pyruvate.
- It produces ATP and NADH, which are used in subsequent steps of cellular respiration.
- The process is divided into energy investment and energy harvesting phases.
