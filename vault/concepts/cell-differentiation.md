# Cell Differentiation and Gene Expression

## Overview
Cell differentiation is the process by which cells become specialized and take on specific functions. This process is tightly controlled by gene regulation, where different subsets of genes are expressed in different cell types, leading to the formation of specialized tissues and organs.

## Key points
- Cell differentiation is a result of differential gene expression, where specific genes are activated in different cell types.
- House-keeping genes are expressed in all cells, while cell-specific genes are expressed in only certain cell types.
- Gene regulation is crucial for the precise control of cell differentiation.
