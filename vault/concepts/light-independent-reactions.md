# Light-Independent Reactions of Photosynthesis

## Overview
The light-independent reactions, also known as the Calvin cycle, are a series of enzyme-mediated reactions that convert carbon dioxide into glucose. This cycle occurs in the stroma of chloroplasts and does not require light directly. It involves the fixation of CO2 onto a 5-carbon sugar, ribulose bisphosphate (RuBP), and the subsequent reduction of the resulting 3-phosphoglycerate (3PG) to glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P).

## Key points
- The Calvin cycle is a light-independent process that occurs in the stroma of chloroplasts
- It involves the fixation of CO2 onto RuBP and the reduction of 3PG to G3P
- The cycle requires ATP and NADPH produced during the light-dependent reactions
