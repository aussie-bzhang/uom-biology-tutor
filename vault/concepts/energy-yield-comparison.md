# Energy Yield Comparison

## Overview
The energy yield from glucose is significantly higher in aerobic respiration (approximately 30 ATPs) compared to anaerobic respiration (2 ATPs from glycolysis and fermentation). This difference highlights the importance of oxygen in energy production.

## Key points
- Aerobic respiration yields about 30 ATPs per glucose
- Anaerobic respiration yields only 2 ATPs per glucose
- The efficiency of ATP production is much higher in aerobic respiration
- Oxygen is essential for the full oxidation of glucose
