# Process of Meiosis

## Overview
Meiosis is a specialized cell division process that reduces the chromosome number by half, resulting in haploid cells. It consists of two rounds of division, meiosis I and meiosis II, which differ from mitosis in their stages and outcomes. During meiosis I, homologous chromosomes pair and segregate, while in meiosis II, sister chromatids separate. This process is crucial for sexual reproduction and genetic diversity.

## Key points
- Meiosis involves two rounds of division, meiosis I and meiosis II.
- Homologous chromosomes pair and segregate during meiosis I.
- Sister chromatids separate during meiosis II.
- Meiosis results in the production of haploid cells.
