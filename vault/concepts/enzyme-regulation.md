# Enzyme Regulation in Metabolic Pathways

## Overview
Enzyme regulation is crucial for controlling the rate of biochemical reactions in metabolic pathways. This regulation can occur through various mechanisms, including changes in enzyme expression, covalent modifications, feedback inhibition, and substrate availability. These mechanisms ensure that metabolic pathways proceed at the appropriate rates to meet cellular needs.

## Key points
- Enzyme regulation is essential for maintaining metabolic balance
- Covalent modifications, such as phosphorylation, can alter enzyme activity
- Feedback inhibition occurs when end products of a pathway inhibit the first enzyme in the pathway
