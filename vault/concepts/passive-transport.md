# Passive Transport

## Overview
Passive transport involves the movement of substances down their concentration gradients, without the input of energy. Diffusion is the normal movement of a substance with its concentration gradient. Osmosis is the diffusion of water across a selectively permeable membrane, while facilitated diffusion involves transport proteins that allow passage of hydrophilic substances across the membrane.

## Key points
- Passive transport moves substances down their concentration gradients.
- Diffusion is the normal movement of a substance with its concentration gradient.
- Osmosis is the diffusion of water across a selectively permeable membrane.
- Facilitated diffusion uses transport proteins for the passage of hydrophilic substances.
