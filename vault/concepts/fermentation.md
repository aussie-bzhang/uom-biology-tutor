# Fermentation

## Overview
Fermentation is an anaerobic process that occurs in the cytoplasm of cells. It uses the end products of glycolysis to regenerate NAD+, allowing glycolysis to continue. However, fermentation yields only a small amount of ATP compared to aerobic respiration.

## Key points
- Fermentation occurs in the absence of oxygen
- It regenerates NAD+ from NADH
- It yields only 2 ATP molecules per glucose
- Types include lactic acid fermentation and alcoholic fermentation
