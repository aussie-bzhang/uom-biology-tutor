# Linked Genes and Recombination

## Overview
Linked genes are those that are located on the same chromosome and tend to be inherited together. Crossing over during meiosis can result in the exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes, creating new allele combinations. The frequency of recombination between linked genes can be used to determine their relative positions on a genetic map, which is useful for predicting the outcomes of genetic crosses.

## Key points
- Linked genes are on the same chromosome and tend to be inherited together.
- Crossing over during meiosis can produce new allele combinations.
- Recombination frequency is a proxy for the distance between genes on a genetic map.
