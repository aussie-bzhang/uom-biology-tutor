# Eukaryotic Cell Cycle

## Overview
The eukaryotic cell cycle consists of interphase and mitosis. Interphase is the longest phase and is divided into G1, S, and G2 phases. Mitosis is the process of nuclear division. The cell cycle is regulated by cyclins and cyclin-dependent kinases, which control progression through the cell cycle.

## Key points
- The cell cycle includes interphase and mitosis
- Interphase accounts for about 90% of the cell cycle
- Cyclins and CDKs regulate the cell cycle
