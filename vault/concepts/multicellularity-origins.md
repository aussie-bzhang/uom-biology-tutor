# Origins of Multicellularity

## Overview
Multicellularity emerged at least six times in eukaryotic history, with evidence suggesting it likely occurred through a series of steps including cell aggregation, intercellular communication, and cell specialization. This transition allowed for increased size, functional specialization, and more complex organisms.

## Key points
- Multicellularity arose at least six times in eukaryotic history.
- The transition to multicellularity likely involved aggregation, communication, and specialization.
- Multicellularity enabled increased size and functional specialization.
