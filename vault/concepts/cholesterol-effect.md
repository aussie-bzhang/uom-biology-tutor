# Effect of Cholesterol on Membrane Permeability

## Overview
Cholesterol is a nonpolar molecule that can form hydrogen bonds with adjacent fatty acid tails of phospholipids. At high temperatures, cholesterol enhances packing of fatty acid tails, reducing permeability. At low temperatures, it disrupts packing, increasing permeability.

## Key points
- Cholesterol forms hydrogen bonds with adjacent fatty acid tails of phospholipids.
- At high temperatures, cholesterol enhances packing, reducing permeability.
- At low temperatures, cholesterol disrupts packing, increasing permeability.
