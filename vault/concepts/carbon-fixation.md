# Carbon Fixation in Photosynthesis

## Overview
Carbon fixation is the process by which atmospheric CO2 is converted into organic molecules. In the Calvin cycle, CO2 is fixed onto RuBP, a 5-carbon sugar, to form 3-phosphoglycerate (3PG). This step is crucial for the synthesis of glucose and other carbohydrates, which are essential for plant growth and energy storage.

## Key points
- RuBP acts as the primary acceptor of CO2 in the Calvin cycle
- The fixation of CO2 is the first step in the conversion of inorganic carbon to organic molecules
- The process is essential for the production of glucose and other carbohydrates
