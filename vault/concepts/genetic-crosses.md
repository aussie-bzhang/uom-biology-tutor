# Genetic Crosses

## Overview
Genetic crosses are essential tools for understanding inheritance patterns. Test crosses help determine the genotype of an unknown parent, back crosses reveal the presence of recessive traits, and reciprocal crosses demonstrate the independence of segregation. These crosses are crucial for interpreting pedigrees and predicting the inheritance of traits.

## Key points
- Test crosses are used to identify the genotype of an unknown parent by crossing it with a homozygous recessive individual.
- Back crosses are performed to determine if a recessive trait is present in a population.
- Reciprocal crosses show that the segregation of alleles is independent of their original pairing.
- Understanding the differences between autosomal and X-linked traits is vital for predicting inheritance patterns.
