# Post-Translational Regulation

## Overview
Post-translational regulation occurs after protein synthesis and involves modifications such as phosphorylation, acetylation, and ubiquitination, which can alter protein activity, stability, and localization.

## Key points
- Phosphorylation is a common post-translational modification that can activate or deactivate enzymes.
- Acetylation can affect protein stability and localization.
- Ubiquitination can target proteins for degradation by the proteasome.
