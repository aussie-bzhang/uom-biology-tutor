# Structure and Function of Enzymes

## Overview
Enzymes are proteins with specific active sites that bind substrates. The active site contains amino acid R groups that interact with the substrate, disrupting its stability and reducing the energy required for the reaction. The chemistry of the active site is more important than its shape in determining enzyme activity.

## Key points
- Enzymes are proteins with specific active sites.
- Amino acid R groups in the active site bind substrates and disrupt their stability.
- The chemistry of the active site is more important than its shape.
