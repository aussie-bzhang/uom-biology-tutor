# Open and Closed Circulatory Systems

## Overview
Open and closed circulatory systems differ in the way fluid moves through the body. Open systems have fluid openly flowing through tissues, while closed systems have fluid enclosed within a network of vessels. Closed systems are more efficient and allow for better control of blood flow.

## Key points
- Open circulatory systems have fluid openly flowing through tissues, while closed systems have fluid enclosed within vessels.
- Closed circulatory systems are more efficient and allow for better control of blood flow.
- Open systems are found in arthropods and non-cephalopod molluscs, while closed systems are common in vertebrates.
