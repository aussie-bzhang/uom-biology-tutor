# Gas Exchange in Plants

## Overview
Plants exchange gases through stomata, small openings on the leaf surface. Carbon dioxide enters the leaf for photosynthesis, while oxygen produced during photosynthesis diffuses out. The spongy mesophyll cells in the leaf blade serve as the specialized exchange surface for gas exchange in plants.

## Key points
- Stomata allow gas exchange in plants
- Spongy mesophyll cells are the specialized exchange surface in plants
- Gas exchange in plants involves the exchange of CO2 and O2
