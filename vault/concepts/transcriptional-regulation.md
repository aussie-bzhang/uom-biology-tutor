# Transcriptional Regulation

## Overview
Transcriptional regulation is the primary mechanism by which gene expression is controlled. It involves the binding of transcription factors to DNA, which can either enhance or inhibit the initiation of transcription.

## Key points
- Transcription factors play a key role in transcriptional regulation.
- Positive regulation involves activators that bind to enhancers, while negative regulation involves repressors that bind to silencers.
- DNA methylation can also regulate transcription by modifying the accessibility of the DNA to transcription factors.
