# Glycogen, Cellulose, and Starch

## Overview
Glycogen, cellulose, and starch are polysaccharides with distinct structures and functions. Glycogen is the primary energy storage molecule in animals, while cellulose forms the structural component of plant cell walls. Starch serves as the primary energy storage in plants and is easily broken down by enzymes.

## Key points
- Glycogen is a branched polysaccharide of α-glucose and is the main energy storage in animals.
- Cellulose is a linear polysaccharide of β-glucose and is the primary structural component of plant cell walls.
- Starch is a branched polysaccharide of α-glucose and is the main energy storage in plants.
