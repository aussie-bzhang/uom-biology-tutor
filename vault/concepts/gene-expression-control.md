# Control of Gene Expression

## Overview
Gene expression is tightly controlled to ensure that the correct amount of protein is produced at the right time and place. This control can occur at various stages, including transcription, mRNA processing, transport, stability, and translation. Understanding these mechanisms is crucial for studying gene regulation and its impact on cellular processes.

## Key points
- Gene expression is regulated to meet the cell's needs for specific proteins.
- Control can occur at multiple levels, including transcriptional and post-transcriptional regulation.
- Positive regulation involves activators that enhance gene expression, while negative regulation involves repressors that inhibit it.
- The lac operon is an example of negative regulation where the LacI repressor controls the expression of genes involved in lactose utilization.
