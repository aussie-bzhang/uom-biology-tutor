# Nucleic Acids: DNA and RNA

## Overview
Nucleic acids, DNA and RNA, are the molecules that store and transmit genetic information. DNA is double-stranded and contains the genetic code, while RNA is single-stranded and plays a crucial role in protein synthesis. Transcription converts DNA into mRNA, and translation converts mRNA into proteins.

## Key points
- DNA is a double-stranded helix with a sugar-phosphate backbone and nitrogenous bases that pair in a specific manner.
- RNA is a single-stranded molecule that can fold into complex structures and is involved in protein synthesis.
- Transcription is the process of copying DNA into mRNA, and translation is the process of converting mRNA into a sequence of amino acids to form a protein.
