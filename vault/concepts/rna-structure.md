# RNA Structure

## Overview
RNA is a single-stranded molecule that can fold into complex structures, which are essential for its function in protein synthesis. Different types of RNA have distinct roles in the cell.

## Key points
- RNA has a sugar-phosphate backbone and nitrogenous bases that can pair with complementary bases.
- RNA can fold into complex structures, such as tRNA and rRNA, which are involved in protein synthesis.
- There are three main types of RNA: mRNA, tRNA, and rRNA.
