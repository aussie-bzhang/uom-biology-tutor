# Lipid-Water Relationship

## Overview
Lipids are crucial for the structure and function of cell membranes due to their hydrophobic nature. They form a lipid bilayer that separates the cell's interior from the external environment, regulating the passage of substances into and out of the cell.

## Key points
- Lipids are hydrophobic and do not interact with water, which is hydrophilic.
- The hydrophobic effect leads to the formation of a lipid bilayer in cell membranes, creating a barrier that controls the cell's environment.
