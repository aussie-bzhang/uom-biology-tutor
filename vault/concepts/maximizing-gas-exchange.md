# Enhancing Gas Exchange

## Overview
The human lung has a unique structure that maximizes gas exchange. Its large surface area, thin barrier, and high partial pressure gradient facilitate efficient diffusion of oxygen and carbon dioxide across the alveoli and capillaries.

## Key points
- Large surface area of the lung maximizes gas exchange
- Thin barrier minimizes diffusion distance
- High partial pressure gradient drives diffusion
