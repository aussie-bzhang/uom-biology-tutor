# Amino Acids: Building Blocks of Proteins

## Overview
Amino acids are the fundamental units of proteins, each containing a central carbon atom with hydrogen, a basic amino group, an acidic carboxyl group, and a variable side chain. The zwitterion form of an amino acid is significant as it can act as both an acid and a base, depending on the pH of the environment.

## Key points
- Amino acids have a central carbon atom with four attached groups: hydrogen, a basic amino group, an acidic carboxyl group, and a side chain.
- The zwitterion form of an amino acid is pH-dependent and can donate or receive protons.
- Amino acids are the monomers that form proteins through peptide bond formation.
