# Temporal and Spatial Regulation of Gene Expression

## Overview
Temporal regulation ensures that genes are expressed at the right time during development, while spatial regulation ensures that genes are expressed in specific cell types or tissues. These mechanisms are critical for the proper functioning of multicellular organisms.

## Key points
- Temporal regulation is essential for developmental processes, while spatial regulation is important for tissue-specific functions.
- Factors such as developmental signals and cell-specific transcription factors influence temporal and spatial regulation.
- The example of the lac operon demonstrates how gene expression can be controlled in response to environmental signals like lactose availability.
