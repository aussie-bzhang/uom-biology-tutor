# Cytoskeleton Structure and Function

## Overview
The cytoskeleton is a network of protein filaments that provides structural support to the cell and is crucial for maintaining cell shape and facilitating cell movement. It is composed of microfilaments, intermediate filaments, and microtubules, each with distinct functions and structures.

## Key points
- The cytoskeleton is a protein-based scaffold within the cytoplasm
- Microfilaments are involved in cell contraction and movement
- Microtubules are involved in cell division and intracellular transport
- Intermediate filaments provide mechanical strength and stability to the cell
