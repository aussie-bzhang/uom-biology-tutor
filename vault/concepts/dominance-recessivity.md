# Dominance and Recessivity

## Overview
Dominance and recessivity describe how different alleles interact to produce a phenotype. Dominant alleles mask the effects of recessive alleles, and a recessive allele is only expressed if it is present in a homozygous state. This concept is fundamental to understanding Mendelian genetics and inheritance patterns.

## Key points
- Dominant alleles mask the effects of recessive alleles.
- A recessive allele is only expressed in a homozygous state.
- Understanding dominance and recessivity is crucial for interpreting genetic crosses and predicting offspring traits.
