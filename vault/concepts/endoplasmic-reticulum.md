# Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)

## Overview
The endoplasmic reticulum is a network of membrane-bound compartments that provides surfaces for the synthesis of proteins, glycoproteins, carbohydrates, and lipids. It is divided into rough ER, which has ribosomes and is involved in protein synthesis, and smooth ER, which lacks ribosomes and is involved in lipid synthesis.

## Key points
- The ER is a network of membrane-bound compartments.
- The rough ER is involved in protein synthesis.
- The smooth ER is involved in lipid synthesis.
