# Allelic Segregation

## Overview
Allelic segregation refers to the separation of different forms of a gene (alleles) during meiosis, which is the basis of Mendel's first law of inheritance. This process ensures that offspring inherit one allele from each parent, leading to genetic diversity. The Punnett square is a tool used to predict the outcomes of genetic crosses based on allelic segregation.

## Key points
- Allelic segregation is the separation of different forms of a gene during meiosis.
- Mendel's first law explains that offspring inherit one allele from each parent.
- The Punnett square is used to predict the outcomes of genetic crosses.
