# Inputs and Outputs of Light-Dependent Reactions

## Overview
The light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis use light energy, water, and carbon dioxide as inputs. They produce oxygen, ATP, and NADPH as outputs. These products are essential for the Calvin cycle, which occurs in the stroma of the chloroplast.

## Key points
- Inputs: light energy, water, carbon dioxide
- Outputs: oxygen, ATP, NADPH
- Products are used in the Calvin cycle
