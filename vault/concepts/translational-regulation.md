# Translational Regulation

## Overview
Translational regulation controls the rate at which mRNA is translated into protein. This regulation can occur at the initiation, elongation, or termination stages of translation.

## Key points
- Initiation factors and ribosomal subunits are involved in the initiation of translation.
- Elongation factors and elongation factors G (eEFGs) are involved in the elongation of the polypeptide chain.
- Termination factors recognize the stop codon and release the completed polypeptide.
