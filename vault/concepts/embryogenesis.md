# Embryogenesis in Multicellular Organisms

## Overview
Embryogenesis is the process by which a multicellular organism forms from a single-celled zygote. It involves precise cell division and differentiation, guided by instructive cues and gene regulation. The establishment of cell fate is a critical step where cells become committed to specific roles in the developing organism.

## Key points
- Embryogenesis is a highly ordered process that results in the formation of specific cell types along major spatial axes.
- Cell fate is established early in embryogenesis, and cells become increasingly restricted in their potential fates.
- Instructive cues and gene regulation play key roles in determining cell fate.
