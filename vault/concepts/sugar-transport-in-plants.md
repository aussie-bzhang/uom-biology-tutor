# Sugar Transport in Plants

## Overview
Sugars are transported through the phloem, a complex system of sieve tube elements and companion cells. The mass flow hypothesis explains how sugars are actively loaded into companion cells and then transported through sieve tube elements.

## Key points
- Sugars are transported through the phloem from source to sink.
- Companion cells provide metabolic support to sieve tube elements.
- The mass flow hypothesis describes the active loading and passive movement of sugars.
