# Dihybrid Cross

## Overview
A dihybrid cross involves two traits controlled by different genes located on different chromosomes. The law of independent assortment dictates that these traits segregate independently during meiosis, leading to predictable ratios of genotypes and phenotypes in the offspring. This concept is fundamental to understanding genetic inheritance and the principles of Mendelian genetics.

## Key points
- Dihybrid crosses demonstrate the independent assortment of alleles from different genes.
- The law of independent assortment predicts the genotypic and phenotypic ratios in the offspring of a dihybrid cross.
- Linked genes on the same chromosome may not assort independently.
