# Test Cross

## Overview
A test cross is used to determine the genotype of an individual with a dominant phenotype. By crossing this individual with a known recessive individual, the genotypes of the offspring can reveal the allelic arrangements of the genes being studied.

## Key points
- Test crosses help identify the genotype of individuals with dominant phenotypes.
- The outcomes of a test cross can reveal the allelic arrangements of linked genes.
- Test crosses are particularly useful when analyzing dihybrid crosses.
