# DNA Structure

## Overview
DNA is a double helix composed of two strands of nucleotides that are connected by hydrogen bonds between complementary bases. The structure of DNA is critical for its function in storing and transmitting genetic information.

## Key points
- DNA has a sugar-phosphate backbone and nitrogenous bases that pair in a specific manner (A-T, C-G).
- The double helix structure allows DNA to be copied accurately during cell division.
- The structure of DNA was elucidated by Watson and Crick in 1953.
