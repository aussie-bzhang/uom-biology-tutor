# Peptide Bonds: Linking Amino Acids

## Overview
Peptide bonds are formed through a condensation reaction, where the carboxyl group of one amino acid reacts with the amino group of another, releasing a water molecule. This process links amino acids together to form a polypeptide chain, with the N-terminus and C-terminus being the ends of the chain.

## Key points
- Peptide bonds are formed by a condensation reaction between the carboxyl and amino groups of adjacent amino acids.
- The N-terminus and C-terminus are the ends of the polypeptide chain, with the N-terminus having an amino group and the C-terminus having a carboxyl group.
