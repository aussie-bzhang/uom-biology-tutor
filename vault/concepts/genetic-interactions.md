# Genetic Interactions

## Overview
Genetic interactions occur when different genes influence the same trait, leading to complex outcomes in genetic crosses. These interactions can be additive, where genes independently contribute to the phenotype, or epistatic, where the expression of one gene is dependent on the presence of another gene.

## Key points
- Genetic interactions result from the influence of multiple genes on a single trait.
- Additive gene interactions occur when genes independently contribute to the phenotype.
- Epistatic gene interactions occur when the expression of one gene is dependent on the presence of another gene.
