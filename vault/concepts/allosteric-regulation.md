# Allosteric Regulation

## Overview
Allosteric regulation involves the binding of a regulatory molecule to a site on the enzyme that is different from the active site, causing a conformational change that affects enzyme activity. This mechanism is crucial for controlling metabolic pathways and maintaining cellular homeostasis.

## Key points
- Allosteric regulation occurs when a regulatory molecule binds to an allosteric site on the enzyme
- Allosteric enzymes can be activated or inhibited by the binding of regulatory molecules
- Allosteric regulation is important for controlling metabolic pathways
