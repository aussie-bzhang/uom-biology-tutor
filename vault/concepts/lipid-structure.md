# Structure of Lipids

## Overview
Lipids are non-polar molecules composed mainly of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. They include triglycerides, which are the primary energy storage molecules, steroids, which have various functions including cell signaling, and phospholipids, which are essential components of cell membranes.

## Key points
- Lipids are non-polar and insoluble in water but dissolve in organic solvents.
- Triglycerides are composed of three fatty acid molecules bonded to a glycerol molecule.
- Phospholipids have a polar head and a non-polar tail, allowing them to form the lipid bilayer of cell membranes.
